wk9_distributions

The Graduate Record Exam (GRE) has a combined verbal and quantitative mean of 1000 and a standard deviation of 200. Scores range from 200 to 1600 and are approximately normally distributed. For each of the following problems draw a Rough Sketch, darkening in the portion of the curve that relates to the answer.

1. What percentage of persons who take the exam score above 1300?

2. What percentage score above 800?

3. What percentage score below 1200?

SAT data are a symmetrical distribution. The scores range from 200-2400. The Mean score is 1300 with a standard deviation of 300.

1) Draw this distribution, making sure to label the standard deviations on your graph.

2) What does the Standard Deviation mean? The standard deviation is the amount the group varies from the mean. I like to think of it as the mean deviation of the mean, because youre doing something similar to a mean calculation by making even cuts of the distribution.

3) If a student scores 2 standard deviations below the mean, what score did they receive? What does this score mean?

Two standard deviations (\(\sigma = 300\)) below a mean of \(\bar X = 1300\) would be 700.

4) If a student scores a 1700, what percentile did the student score in? What does this mean?

pnorm(1700,mean = 1300, sd = 300)

[1] 0.9087888

This would put a student scoring 1700 at just above the 90th percentile (~91st). That means that this student scored better than 91% of the sampled group. Alternatively, only 9% of students scored better than 1700.

5) What percentile would a student who scores a 1000 be in?

pnorm(1000,mean = 1300, sd = 300)

[1] 0.1586553

Around the 16th percentile.

6) What percentage of students score above 1900?

1-pnorm(1900,mean = 1300, sd = 300)

[1] 0.02275013

Just 2% of students scored over 1900.
